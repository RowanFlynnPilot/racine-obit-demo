In Memoriam

Darlene Chambers

1958 – 2026

Darlene Chambers passed away on June 14, 2026, in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, at the age of 68. She was born on February 3, 1958, in Racine, Wisconsin, and spent her life rooted in the community she called home. Darlene’s passing came as a result of a heart attack, leaving behind a legacy of love, strength, and cherished memories that will remain with all who knew her.

Darlene was a proud and devoted mother to her sons, Tony Chambers, Jermal Chambers, and Darrius Chambers, who were among the greatest joys of her life. She was also a loving grandmother to Tony Chambers Jr., Aminah Chambers, and Aidan Majors, and she treasured the time she spent with her grandchildren. Her family was the center of her world, and she embraced every role she held with grace, loyalty, and a deep sense of care. She is lovingly remembered by her significant other, Edward Dawson, who shared in her life and companionship.

She is survived by her sisters, Sakina Abdullah, Belinda Chambers/Cooper and her husband Charles Cooper, and Rajeeyah McWhorter and her husband Andre McWhorter, as well as her brothers, Dante Chambers and Hannibal Chambers. Darlene was preceded in death by her beloved siblings, Yvonne Jennings and Sherman Chambers, and by her parents, Aminah Sabir, also known as Herriata Chambers, and Nathaniel Chambers. Her family ties were strong and meaningful, and she carried those bonds with her throughout her life. She also leaves behind a host of nieces and nephews who will remember her warmth, her presence, and the love she showed so freely.

Darlene attended Park High School in Racine, where she began building the foundation for the life she would go on to lead. She was a hardworking woman who dedicated more than 20 years of her life to serving others as a CNA in the healthcare industry. Her work reflected her compassionate nature and her willingness to care for people with dignity and respect. She retired after many years of faithful service, having touched countless lives through her dedication and kindness. Her commitment to helping others was one of the many ways she demonstrated the strength of her character.

Those who knew Darlene will remember her as a very sociable person who loved being around family and friends. She had a vibrant personality and carried herself with confidence and style. She was outgoing, hardworking, and fashionable, and she enjoyed spending time with the people she loved. Darlene especially loved the Packers and enjoyed watching them play, a pastime that brought her joy and excitement. She found happiness in simple moments shared with friends, and her lively spirit made those moments memorable for everyone around her.

Darlene’s life was one of connection, devotion, and resilience. She will be remembered for her loving heart, her strong presence, and the way she made others feel welcomed and valued. Her memory will live on in the hearts of her children, grandchildren, siblings, extended family, friends, and all who were blessed to know her. Though her time on earth has come to an end, the love she gave and the life she lived will continue to shine through the many lives she touched.

Arrangements by Urquhart Crenshaw Funeral Home and Cremation.
