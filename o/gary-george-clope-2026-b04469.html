In Memoriam

Gary George Clope

2026

Gary George Clope, “Zipper,” 89, of Racine, died Thursday, June 18, 2026, at his residence.

Gary George Clope’s Early Life

Gary was born Sept. 29, 1936, in Racine, the son of the late Clarence and Marie (née Dreher) Clope. He graduated from St. Catherine’s High School, Class of 1954.

On Nov. 16, 1957, at St. Florian Catholic Church in Milwaukee, Gary was united in marriage to Suzanne Marie Becker. They first met while attending a St. Catherine’s vs. Pius basketball game in Racine, beginning a love story that would span nearly seven decades.

Gary worked for Wisconsin Electric Company for 37 years, retiring in 1994 as a dispatcher. After retirement, he continued working at Ericksons Auto Trim for 15 years. He also worked as a bartender at Douglas Bowl for 30 years. When he wasn’t behind the bar, he could be found on the lanes; he was an avid bowler.

Gary was a member of St. Rita Catholic Church for more than 60 years. He enjoyed playing sports and was part of the championship DeRango’s 12-inch softball team in the 1960s. He was a passionate sports fan, especially of the Green Bay Packers , holding season tickets since the 1950s.

After retirement, Gary and Suzanne enjoyed spending their winters in Venice, Florida, where they made many friendships and memories. When he was not watching sports, he spent countless hours gardening. He was also known for never missing his daily “medicine” — a gin martini promptly at 5 o’clock. Above all, he treasured time with his family and will be remembered as a devoted husband, father, grandfather and great-grandfather.

Survived by

Gary is survived by his wife of 68 years, Suzanne; children David (Jane) Clope, Scott (Lauri) Clope, Gail (Robert) Stedman, Laura Black, and Jane (Ian) Connaughton; 11 grandchildren Hayley, Matthew, Jacob, Nicholas, Kathleen, Meaghann, Clara, Benjamin, Elizabeth, Lucas and Morgan; 10.5 great-grandchildren; and nieces, nephews, other relatives and friends.

In addition to his parents, Gary was preceded in death by his sister and brother-in-law, Gretchen and Pat Dechant.

Memorial Services

A Mass of Christian Burial will be celebrated at 11 a.m. Tuesday, June 30, 2026, at St. Rita Catholic Church, 4339 Douglas Ave., with Fr. Mark Obeten officiating. Relatives and friends may meet with the family from 10 to 11 a.m. Tuesday at the church. Private interment will take place at Holy Cross Cemetery, Highway 32, at a later date.

Memorials to St. Rita Catholic Church and/or St. Catherine’s High School have been suggested.

The family extends thanks to Heartland Hospice nurse Kyla, as well as the aides at Parkview Gardens Assisted Living, especially Susan, Maria, Temeka, Vicky, Meg, Maurice and Kevin.
