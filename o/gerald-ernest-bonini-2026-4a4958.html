In Memoriam

Gerald Ernest Bonini

1941 – 2026

Gerald Ernest Bonini, “Jerry,” 84, of Kenosha, Wis., formerly of Racine, died Saturday, June 27, 2026, at Hospice House.

Gerald Ernest Bonini’s Early Life

He was born Oct. 18, 1941, in Racine, the son of the late Ernest Francis and Emma Frances (Lucarelli) Bonini.

He was raised in Racine and attended Holy Trinity elementary school. He lived in Milwaukee from 1954 to 1963 while attending De Sales Preparatory School and St. Francis Seminary, where he received a bachelor’s degree in philosophy. He lived in Chicago from 1964 to 1965 while attending Loyola University graduate school, where he worked as a teaching assistant to underclass students and earned a master’s degree in English.

He married Carol Jean (Nelson) Bonini on Nov. 25, 1965.

He was employed as an English teacher at Bradford Senior High School from 1965 to 1978; at First National Bank of Racine from 1963 to 1971; at Kenosha National Bank (later First Bank Southeast, then Firstar Bank) from 1971 to 1994; and at Southport Bank from 1997 until his retirement in 2006. He began his banking career as a part-time teller and retired as executive vice president and chief operating officer of Southport Bank.

He served as a Kenosha transit commissioner from 1985 to 1991 and served as co-chairman of the Shalom Center for the Interfaith Network from 2003 to 2008. He also served as treasurer and president of the Somers Estates Condominium Owners Association from 2009 to 2015, and from 2018 until 2020. He was a member of the Blood Center and contributed more than 100 pints of blood. He served on church, education and banking committees and commissions. His interests included family genealogy, reading, puzzle solving, cooking and photography.

Survived by

He is survived by his wife, Carol; three children, Ann Marie (Phillip Edward) Lambert of Fort Worth, Texas, Karen Lynn (Mark Anthony) Fetherston of Racine, and Anthony Mark Bonini (Tracy Bynum) of Kenosha; five grandchildren, Joseph Brian Lambert, K’Lynn Mackenzie Lambert, Cassidy Grace Lambert, Stuart Cameron Fetherston and Nathan Emerson Fetherston; his sister, Theresa Rose (Donald Roy) Carey; his brother-in-law, Paul Edward Nelson; sisters-in-law Jane Ann Norbin and Mary Ellen Goldsworthy; and nieces, nephews and cousins.

He was preceded in death by his parents; his sister, Carol Ann Bonini Neufeld; his brother-in-law, John Franz Neufeld; his in-laws, Norman Herbert Nelson and Margaret Jean (Phillips) Nelson; his brother-in-law, Jack Merrill Goldsworthy; his sister-in-law, Teresa Ann Nelson; and his brother-in-law, Donald Joseph Norbin.

Services

A gathering will be held Monday, July 13, 2026, at St. Elizabeth Catholic Church, 714 49th St., Kenosha, WI 53140, from 8 to 10 a.m. A Mass of Christian burial will follow at 10 a.m. Private inurnment will follow.

In lieu of flowers, memorials may be made to Hospice Alliance, 10220 Prairie Ridge Blvd., Pleasant Prairie, WI 53158 ( https://www.hospicealliance.org ), or to the Shalom Center, 4314 39th Ave., Kenosha, WI 53144 ( https://www.shalomcenter.org/donate ).
