In Memoriam

1st Lt. Steven Kojis

1992 – 2026

It is with deep love and profound sadness that we announce the passing of our beloved son, brother and uncle, Steven Kojis, who suffered a catastrophic brain injury during a prolonged cardiac arrest while being treated for a brief and sudden illness while on active duty in the US Air Force in San Antonio, Texas.

Steven was born and raised in the Burlington area and made memories with countless friends and family. Steven’s gentle and empathetic soul, combined with his silly humor and adventurous spirit, manifested itself in his interesting and varied hobbies and talents, along with making friends of all ages and from many places. Steven loved with his whole heart.

In first grade, Steven drew a picture of what he wanted to be when he grew up, which was of himself saluting by a flag, saying,”I want to serve my country.” He enlisted in the Air Force in his senior year of high school, and dedicated himself to doing just that for his entire career.

Steven was quick to find a new hobby wherever the military took him, from fishing, caring for horses, and line dancing at Tinker AFB in Oklahoma, to autobahn driving, hiking, and wine making at Ramstein AFB in Germany. He enjoyed working on home improvement projects including installing hard wood flooring, gardening, yard fencing and trench digging (well, maybe not so much the trench digging) in Maryland while he was stationed at the Pentagon, to river walking and Veo biking while stationed at Lackland AFB in Texas, among so many others.

Steven’s accomplishments in the Air Force started early. He promoted quickly, and often earned awards for his integrity and his leadership as a young Airman. He was proud to work in the office of the Secretary of the Air Force while stationed at the Pentagon - a position well earned. While working at the Pentagon, he earned his Bachelor’s of Business Administration from the University of Maryland GC. He was then selected to attend Officer Training School and was commissioned as an officer. He was a deeply dedicated Airman and worked beyond expectation in every duty.

Steven was devoted to his Catholic faith and was a member of St. Mary parish in his hometown of Burlington.

Steven will be profoundly missed by his mother, Babette Montee; sister and brother-in-law Elizabeth and Michael Grace; sister, Summer Peterson; nephews Michael, Jack, and Jameson Grace and niece Adelyn Grace; and by countless friends and extended family.

The family welcome all who loved Steven to join them for a visitation at the Daniels Family Funeral Home on Thursday, June 18, 2026 from 4:00-8:00pm, with a Mass of Christian Burial on Friday, June 19 at 10:30 am at St. Mary Catholic Church in Burlington, followed by full military honors at St. Mary Cemetery. We also welcome friends and family for a relaxed picnic reception at Bushnell Park Friday afternoon.

The family would like to extend our deepest gratitude to the 37th Training Group, among many others, for their unwavering support of Steven and his family during this devastating experience. They are his Air Force family and we share our grief together as one united family.

Eternal rest grant unto him, O Lord,

and let perpetual light shine upon him.

May the souls of all the faithful departed,

through the mercy of God, rest in peace.

Amen
