In Memoriam

Roger John Hayek

1935 – 2026

Roger John Hayek, 91, of Racine, died Tuesday, June 16, 2026, at Ridgewood Care Center .

Roger John Hayek’s Early Life

He was born March 8, 1935, in Racine, the son of the late John and Iva (née Stephan) Hayek. He graduated from Wm. Horlick High School , Class of 1953.

Roger enlisted in the U.S. Army and served 26 months stationed in Tokyo, Japan. After returning to Racine, he met Jana L. Clausen at UW Extension. They were married Feb. 1, 1958, at Atonement Lutheran Church.

Roger graduated from the University of Wisconsin–Parkside, received teaching credits through Carthage College, and earned a master’s degree from the University of Wisconsin–Whitewater. He worked for Racine Unified, Educators Credit Union, and Racine Federated as a construction tool salesman.

Roger loved sailing on Brown’s Lake and played tennis and racquetball into his later years. In retirement, he also enjoyed biking, swimming, cribbage and sheepshead. He will be remembered as a devoted husband, father, grandfather and great-grandfather.

He is survived by his wife of 68 years, Jana; children Scott (Cheryl) Hayek, Debra (Jeff) Minton and Kristi (Peter White) Hayek; grandchildren Bradley Hayek, Christen (Steve) Schommer, Lyndsay (Mark) Haubrich and Taylor (Jenna) Minton; great-grandchildren Leo, Camden and Beau; Daniel, Charles and Sophia; and Jordan, Logan and Weston; sister-in-law Judith Clausen; and nieces, nephews, other relatives and friends.

In addition to his parents, Roger was preceded in death by his sister and brothers-in-law Shirley (George) Kamakian and William Clausen.

Memorial Service

A memorial service will be held at 11 a.m. Wednesday, July 8, 2026, at Maresh-Meredith & Acklam Funeral Home, 803 Main St. Relatives and friends may meet with the family from 10 to 11 a.m. Entombment with full military honors will follow at Southern Wisconsin Veterans Memorial Cemetery.

Memorials to St. Jude Children’s Research Hospital have been suggested.

The family thanks the Compassus Hospice nurses and aides for their loving and compassionate care.
