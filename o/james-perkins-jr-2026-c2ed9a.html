In Memoriam

James Perkins Jr

1956 – 2026

James Perkins Jr., known to friends and loved ones simply as James, died on June 18, 2026, in Wisconsin after a heart attack, bringing to a close a life that began on November 18, 1956, in Greenwood, Mississippi.

James was remembered as a man whose presence could lift a room with ease. He was funny in a way that came naturally, never forced, and he had a gift for making people laugh even in difficult moments. Those who knew him best say his humor was matched by a deep sense of passion and a thoughtful approach to life that made him both memorable and dependable. He carried himself with warmth, and he left an impression that lasted long after a conversation ended.

Born and raised in Greenwood, Mississippi, James grew up with the values of hard work, resilience, and community that shaped his outlook for the rest of his life. He took those early lessons with him wherever he went, and they became part of the way he treated others. He understood the importance of listening, showing up, and offering encouragement when it mattered most. That steady character made him a trusted friend and a respected presence among those around him.

Music was one of James’s greatest interests, and it remained a meaningful part of his life through the years. Whether he was listening, sharing a favorite song, or simply appreciating the power of a good melody, music gave him joy and connection. It reflected something essential about who he was. He appreciated rhythm, soul, and expression, and that appreciation often mirrored the way he lived his own life. He moved through the world with feeling, energy, and a genuine love for the moments that brought people together.

James was also known for being thoughtful in ways that made a difference. He paid attention to details, remembered what mattered to people, and often offered words that were both sincere and timely. His passion was not loud for its own sake. It was steady, meaningful, and rooted in care. He believed in giving his best, and that quality could be seen in the way he spoke, the way he worked, and the way he treated others with dignity and respect.

As news of his passing spread, those who knew James reflected on a life that was defined not only by the years he lived, but by the spirit he brought to those years. He will be remembered for his humor, his heart, and the sincerity he brought to every relationship. He was the kind of person who made an ordinary day feel brighter and a difficult day feel more manageable. That is a legacy that endures.

James Perkins Jr. died at the age of 69, and his life stands as a reminder of the lasting value of kindness, laughter, and genuine connection. He leaves behind memories that will continue to speak for him, carrying forward the sound of his laughter, the strength of his passion, and the quiet wisdom of his thoughtful nature. In the end, James will be remembered not for how he left this world, but for how fully he lived in it.

Arrangements by Urquhart Crenshaw Funeral Home and Cremation.
